Reading it as a risk person. This is a reliability diagram — the same tool you'd use to validate any probabilistic model. Brier and log loss are proper scoring rules; calibration bias is the average signed gap between what happened and what was priced. Buckets are thin (often 15–30 forecasts), so treat mid-range deviations as suggestive over a single tournament, not established — the Wilson bands show how wide that uncertainty runs. This is process insight, not a deployable edge.
